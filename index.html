Who we are

A small charity with a simple job

Favela Brass teaches brass and percussion, free of charge, to children and teenagers in Rio de Janeiro. Founded in 2014 by the British trumpeter Tom Ashe, it has grown from a handful of students in the Pereira da Silva favela into a music school working across the city, with former students now on its teaching staff.

Friends of Favela Brass is its supporters' charity in the UK. We were registered with the Charity Commission in 2021 so that donations from Britain could reach the school efficiently and grow by 25% through Gift Aid. We are run entirely by volunteer trustees; nobody here is paid a penny.

We don't run programmes ourselves. We buy the things the school needs and fund the opportunities its students earn. It is not a complicated model, but it works.

What we fund

Where your money actually goes

We keep our costs at zero and our purchases concrete. Recent examples, all agreed and minuted by the trustees:

Instruments

Instruments and the unglamorous essentials

From trumpets to this year's baritone saxophone, plus the things nobody photographs: cases, gig bags, reeds, repairs and spare parts, sourced in the UK or in Brazil, whichever is cheaper.

Exams

Graded music exam fees

Favela Brass students sit graded exams with MTB, a UK exam board, earning internationally recognised qualifications. We pay the entry fees, one student and one grade at a time.

Instrument donations

Second-hand instruments, second lives

Have a trumpet gathering dust in the loft? We take donated instruments, have them repaired here in the UK, and get them to Rio. Do email us first so we can plan the journey.

Documentation

Recording and film equipment

Sound recorders, microphones and lighting so the school can document its performances properly, and so you can see where your money went.

Exchange

Cultural exchange

Our founding objects include mutual learning between young musicians in Brazil and Europe. In 2026 we helped fund travel for Favela Brass's first exchange visit to Germany.

The school we support

Favela Brass, Rio de Janeiro

An independent Brazilian association with its own board, teachers and funding. Here is what your donations are part of:

~200students learning free of charge
5partner state schools
50+performances a year

Students progress from beginner workshops in state schools to a structured advanced course, playing everything from street carnival to the Copacabana Palace. Several of today's teachers came up through the project themselves.
